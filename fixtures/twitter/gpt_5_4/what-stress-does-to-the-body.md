1/10 Stress is not just a mood. It is a body-wide alarm system. Useful in a real emergency, expensive when it stays on all day.

2/10 When stress sticks around, your brain keeps pumping out signals that raise adrenaline and cortisol. That can leave you tense, wired, tired, and weirdly bad at resting.

3/10 Sleep is usually one of the first things to get hit. You fall asleep later, wake up more, or sleep long enough and still feel like a phone charger with a loose cable.

4/10 Your heart feels it too. Stress can push heart rate and blood pressure up in the moment. If that becomes your daily setting, your body pays for it.

5/10 Your gut is not sitting this one out. Stress can mess with appetite, trigger nausea, tighten your stomach, and turn digestion into a full-time complaint department.

6/10 Muscles keep score. Jaw clenching, tight shoulders, tension headaches, random aches, that is often your nervous system staying on guard long after the threat is gone.

7/10 Your brain gets noisier under chronic stress. Focus drops. Memory gets worse. Small problems feel larger, and ordinary tasks start feeling like admin from hell.

8/10 Immunity can take a hit too. When your body is spending all day in alarm mode, it has less room for repair, recovery, and fighting off every bug going around.

9/10 This is why stress can look like ten different problems at once: bad sleep, stomach issues, irritability, pain, fatigue, brain fog. The body loves a group project when things go wrong.

10/10 What helps is boring, which is rude but true: regular sleep, movement, less alcohol, fewer open loops, and actual recovery time. If symptoms keep piling up, get checked. Your body is trying to tell you the load is too high.
