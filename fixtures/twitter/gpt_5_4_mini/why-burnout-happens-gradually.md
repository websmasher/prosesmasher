1/10 Burnout does not usually show up like a brick through a window. It starts as a few small extra asks that seem fine in the moment.

2/10 One late night. One skipped break. One more week of saying yes. That is how the slide begins.

3/10 Early burnout can look like commitment. You are still answering fast, still helping, still holding it together.

4/10 The catch is that your body notices what your calendar hides. Sleep gets worse. Patience gets thinner. Focus starts to wobble.

5/10 Burnout grows when demand stays high and recovery stays low. Too much work, too little control, and too little support is a bad setup for any human.

6/10 Most people wait for a big crash. The more common pattern is erosion. Energy drops first. Then motivation. Then the sense that the work matters.

7/10 The danger is that you can get used to tired. Then tired becomes normal. Then normal becomes the new baseline, and everyone calls it “busy.”

8/10 Burnout also gets worse when the job keeps changing shape. More urgency, more responsibility, more open loops, but no real change in time or staffing.

9/10 Quick fixes help for a day. A day off helps. A quieter week helps. But a broken workload comes back on Monday.

10/10 If work has felt heavier for weeks, take it seriously before it becomes full collapse. Burnout is easier to interrupt early than to recover from after months of running on fumes.
