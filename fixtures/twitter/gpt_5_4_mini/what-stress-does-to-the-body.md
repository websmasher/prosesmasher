1/10 Stress is not just a feeling. It is the body’s alarm system, and it changes how you sleep, think, digest, and recover.

2/10 Short bursts of stress can help you react fast. The problem starts when the alarm stays on for days, weeks, or months.

3/10 Chronic stress keeps hormones like adrenaline and cortisol working overtime. That can leave your heart rate up, your muscles tight, and your sleep lighter.

4/10 It also hits the brain. Stress can make you more irritable, less focused, and more likely to forget simple things.

5/10 The gut feels it too. Stress can show up as nausea, stomach pain, diarrhea, constipation, or appetite changes.

6/10 Your immune system can take a hit. People under constant stress often feel run down more easily and bounce back more slowly.

7/10 Muscle tension is one of the most common signs. Jaw clenching, neck pain, shoulder aches, and headaches are often stress with a body costume on.

8/10 Stress can also push habits sideways. More alcohol, less movement, and worse food choices can become part of the cycle.

9/10 What helps? Sleep on a schedule, move every day, cut back on alcohol, and make the next task smaller. Tiny changes still count.

10/10 If stress is messing with work, sleep, relationships, or health, do not shrug it off. Get help before your body starts filing complaints in louder ways.
