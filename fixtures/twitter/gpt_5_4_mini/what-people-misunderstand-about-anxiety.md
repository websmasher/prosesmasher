1/10 Anxiety is a normal alarm system. It helps you notice danger, get ready, and act.

2/10 The mistake is treating every anxious feeling as a sign something is broken. Anxiety is a body state, not a moral failure.

3/10 Anxiety disorders are different. NIMH says the fear does not go away, shows up in many situations, and can get worse over time.

4/10 Anxiety does not stay in the mind. It can show up as poor sleep, trouble concentrating, muscle tension, stomach problems, and a racing heart.

5/10 That is why "just calm down" misses the point. If the alarm is stuck on, the body does not care how tidy the advice sounds.

6/10 Avoidance makes anxiety louder. Skipping the meeting, the drive, or the phone call gives short relief, then teaches the brain the situation was dangerous.

7/10 Panic can be confusing. A sudden wave of fear can feel like a medical emergency, which is one reason panic disorder gets missed.

8/10 Treatment is practical, not mystical. CBT and gradual exposure are core tools, and some people also benefit from medication through a clinician.

9/10 If anxiety is lasting, severe, or getting in the way of sleep, work, or relationships, get help. If someone is in crisis or thinking about self-harm, urgent support matters now.

10/10 Anxiety is common. Untreated anxiety is exhausting. The goal is not to never feel it, it is to stop living at its mercy.
