Most habits do not fail because you lack discipline. They fail because **willpower is a terrible system**.

Willpower is expensive. It drops when you are tired, stressed, rushed, sick, distracted, or one bad email away from eating cereal over the sink at 9:40 p.m. If your habit only works on your best days, it does not work. It is just a fantasy with a calendar reminder.

That is why people set a big goal, feel fired up for a week, then quietly slide back into the old pattern. The plan was built on emotion, not structure. Motivation got the grand opening. Nothing stayed behind to run the store.

Habits hold when the cue is clear and the first step is small. After coffee, take the vitamin. After brushing your teeth, floss one tooth. After opening the laptop, write one sentence before touching email. Small is not weak. Small is repeatable.

You also need less friction. Put the book on the pillow. Leave the shoes by the door. Fill the water bottle before bed. Make the good choice a little easier and the default choice does not have to win every argument.

Then plan for imperfect days. Missing once is normal. The damage starts when one miss becomes a speech about your character. Skip the speech. Do the next rep.

If a habit depends on feeling inspired, it will wobble. If it depends on a stable cue, a small action, and a setup that helps you start, it has a real chance.
