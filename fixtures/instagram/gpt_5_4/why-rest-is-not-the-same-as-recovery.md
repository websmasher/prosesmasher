A lot of people say they rested all weekend, then wake up on Monday feeling like a phone that charged to 12%.

That is because **rest and recovery are not the same job**.

Rest is stopping. Recovery is rebuilding.

You can stop working and still stay mentally on call. You can lie on the couch while replaying a hard conversation, checking email, scrolling for two hours, or bracing for the week ahead. Your body reads that as more load, not repair.

Recovery is what helps the stress system come down. It usually looks less glamorous than people want. Good sleep. Time without demands. Movement that loosens the body instead of punishing it. Food that keeps you steady. Quiet. Play. Being with people who do not drain you. Doing one thing at a time instead of seventeen half-broken things.

This is why a packed vacation can leave you tired. It is why a Sunday full of errands, doomscrolling, and low-grade dread does not feel restorative. And it is why burnout does not disappear just because you took a day off.

If you feel constantly tired, ask a better question than “Did I rest?”

Ask:

Did my body get a chance to unclench?
Did my brain get a break from input?
Did I have any time that was not useful to someone else?

Recovery is less about escape and more about nervous system repair.

Sometimes the fix is not more productivity hacks. It is protecting sleep, shrinking the noise, saying no earlier, and letting your body believe the threat has passed.
